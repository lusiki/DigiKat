# Literature map — Inflation salience & CST framing in Croatian Catholic media

**Date:** 2026-06-29 · via `/lit-review` (3 parallel search agents) · APA 7th
**Status:** first pass. ⚠️ Items tagged UNVERIFIED need one confirming lookup (Hrčak / CrossRef / OUP) before
they enter `refs.bib`. Do NOT cite UNVERIFIED items as settled.

This study sits at the intersection of three literatures. The point of the map is to show that each one is
well-developed *on its own* but **none meets the other two** — which is the gap.

## Gap statement (the one-sentence version)

> The inflation-salience literature measures *secular* media attention against real CPI; the CST literature
> theorizes a *structural-vs-charity* economic frame; the Croatian literature documents an *identity-driven*
> Catholic media field — **but no study has put the three together: whether Croatian Catholic media's
> attention to inflation (2021–2025) tracks the components that hit the poor (food/energy), and whether it
> frames that hardship *structurally* (CST critique) or *charitably* (Caritas appeal).**

**Closest prior work:** Tranfić (2022) — frame analysis of Croatian Catholic discourse, but on gender, not
economics; and Aarab et al. (2025) — a media inflation-attention index, but secular and non-Croatian. This
study is the first to occupy the overlap.
